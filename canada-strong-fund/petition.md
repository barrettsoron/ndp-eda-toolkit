## Petition Statement

We, the undersigned residents of [EDA name] and surrounding communities, call on the federal government to:

**1. Reject asset recycling as a path to capitalize the Canada Strong Fund.** Do not sell off Trans Mountain Corporation, federally-owned port lands, the public stake in Hibernia, Crown real estate, or any other public asset to fund minority equity stakes in private projects. Public assets stay public.

**2. Fund the Canada Strong Fund through a windfall profits tax on oil and gas.** Canadian oil and gas companies are projected to clear $90 billion in profits this year, on the back of price spikes driven by Trump's war in Iran. That is the revenue source for a real Canadian sovereign wealth fund — not $25 billion in taxpayer cash transferred to a Bay Street–managed Crown corporation.

We are concerned that the Canada Strong Fund as announced on April 27, 2026:

- **Inverts the sovereign wealth fund model** Norway and Alaska have made famous — using public money to subsidize private projects, instead of using industry profits to support the public good;
- **Carries no climate conditions, no UNDRIP/FPIC requirements, and no union-content or Canadian-content thresholds** in its written mandate, despite spoken commitments to the contrary;
- **Was designed in consultation with Bay Street** — Finance Minister Champagne briefed the country's largest banks, pension plans, and construction companies on the morning of the announcement; labour, Indigenous nations, and climate organizations were not at the equivalent table;
- **Repeats the structural problems of the Canada Infrastructure Bank** — same arm's-length structure, same Bay Street board, same "private leverage" claims the Parliamentary Budget Officer has been unable to verify, with a bigger price tag.

Norway uses industry profits to support the public good. The Canada Strong Fund as designed uses public money to support private profits. **We call on the House of Commons to amend the Canada Strong Fund's enabling legislation** to fund the fund through a windfall profits tax on oil and gas, prohibit asset recycling of Crown corporations and public infrastructure, and write binding climate, FPIC, and union-content conditions into the statute.

A real people's fund taxes the rents flowing to the wealthiest corporations and redistributes them to the public good. Anything less is the Liberals using progressive language to do Bay Street's work.

**Pull a Norway.**
