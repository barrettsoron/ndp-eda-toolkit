## Context

The downtown and Mount Pleasant sites both sit on **unceded** territories of the **xʷməθkʷəy̓əm (Musqueam), Skwxwú7mesh (Squamish), and səlilwətaɬ (Tsleil-Waututh)** Nations. Unlike Treaty 4 (Regina), and unlike the Peace and Friendship Treaties (Saint John), no land in this region was ever surrendered by treaty. The unceded framing is more than a land acknowledgement — it shapes the legal and political terrain a campaign has to operate on. UNDRIP-aligned consultation (FPIC) is the floor, not a ceiling.

The Kamloops site sits on **Tk'emlúps te Secwépemc** territory (Secwepemcúl'ecw) — that conversation is for the Kamloops–Thompson–Cariboo or Kamloops–Shuswap–Central Rockies EDA to lead, not Vancouver Centre.

## Do / don't

*[Mirror Regina structure. Key:*
- *Don't speak for the Nations. Don't assume opposition. Don't assume support.*
- *Don't drop "unceded" into copy without ground-truthing the framing the Nations themselves use in current public statements.*
- *Don't conflate the three Nations — they have distinct governance, distinct relationships to the city, and distinct positions on infrastructure projects.*
- *Coordinate with Vancouver East NDP and (for the Kamloops site) the relevant interior EDA before reaching out — multiple ridings approaching the same Nations uncoordinated is the exact pattern to avoid.]*
