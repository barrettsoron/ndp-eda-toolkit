Hi [Name],

You may have seen the news: Bell Canada is building what it calls Canada's largest AI data centre campus — 300 megawatts on 160 acres in the RM of Sherwood, just south of Regina. The announced investment is $1.7 billion. The anchor tenants are CoreWeave and Cerebras — both American AI companies.

Here's what that means in plain terms.

**Eighty permanent jobs.** Not eight hundred — that's the temporary construction figure. The permanent workforce is 80 people. For context, a single medium-sized grocery store employs more people than that.

**A massive new load on our grid.** Saskatchewan's electricity comes from roughly 80% fossil fuels. SaskEnergy is already building a new natural gas pipeline specifically to serve this facility. In Virginia, where data centres clustered at scale, residential electricity bills doubled. Every ratepayer in the province is exposed to that risk.

**A governance crisis.** Four of seven RM of Sherwood council members resigned shortly after the announcement. The province appointed interim replacements. Whatever happened in that process, it wasn't democratic.

**American tenants, Canadian soil.** Under US law, American authorities can compel access to data held by US companies regardless of where it's stored. And these aren't neutral tech firms. CoreWeave started as a cryptocurrency mining company run by hedge fund traders. Cerebras has active DARPA military contracts building battlefield simulation systems for the US Navy, CIA venture funding, and an investor roster that includes Rebekah Mercer's "America First" fund. These are the companies we're reshaping Saskatchewan's grid for.

NDP Leader Avi Lewis has committed to a [**Humans-First AI Policy**](https://lewisisleader.ca/ideas/dignified-work) that includes **pausing data centre expansion** until communities have been properly consulted and environmental impacts independently assessed. Our clean water and affordable energy should serve people first.

**What we're doing locally:**

We're collecting petition signatures across Regina–Wascana calling on the federal government to pause data centre expansion until democratic oversight catches up. This is a community-rooted initiative — your riding association, your neighbours, your signatures.

**How you can help:**

- **Sign the petition** at your next local NDP event or contact us to arrange pickup of signature sheets.
- **Canvass your neighbourhood** — we have a canvasser guide with talking points, common objections, and instructions. No experience needed.
- **Share on social media** — we have ready-to-post copy for Twitter/X, Facebook, and Instagram. Just ask.
- **Talk to your neighbours** — the fact sheet is a good leave-behind for anyone who wants to learn more.

If you'd like canvassing materials, signature sheets, or social copy, reply to this email and we'll get you set up.

This is one of those issues where the numbers tell the story: $1.7 billion for Bell and two American AI companies. Eighty jobs and a gas pipeline for us. That's not a fair deal — and the petition is how we say so on the record.
