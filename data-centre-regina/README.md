# Data Centre Petition — Regina–Wascana NDP

Assets for the community campaign to pause Bell Canada's 300 MW AI data centre in the RM of Sherwood, outside Regina, Saskatchewan.

**Bell** is building what it calls Canada's largest purpose-built AI data centre campus — 160 acres, $1.7 billion, serving American AI companies CoreWeave and Cerebras. NDP Leader Avi Lewis has committed to a [Humans-First AI Policy](https://lewisisleader.ca/ideas/dignified-work) that includes pausing data centre expansion to protect clean water, affordable energy, and community decision-making.

This is an riding-level initiative on **Treaty 4 territory**. All materials use NDP federal branding.

## E-Petition

These materials support **physical signature collection only**. To set up an electronic petition:

- **Through ndp.ca:** Work with your regional organizer to request an e-petition page on the federal NDP platform. This gives you centralized data collection, NDP branding, and integration with the national CRM.
- **Locally:** Build your own using a privacy-respecting form tool. Ensure you comply with the federal NDP Privacy Policy and Canadian anti-spam legislation (CASL) for any email follow-up. Signatures collected electronically should follow the same data custody protocols as physical forms.

Either way, the petition copy in `petition.md` can be adapted for digital use.

---

## Data Custody

The Regina–Wascana NDP electoral district association has custody of all data collected through this petition, governed by the federal NDP Privacy Policy.

- Personal information is used to contact signatories about NDP activities and shared with riding associations as needed.
- Personal data is **never sold**.
- Signatories can opt out at any time: dnc@ndp.ca | ndp.ca/privacy
- Canvassers must **not** photograph, screenshot, or store personal data on personal devices.
- Signed paper petitions should be returned to chapter leads or brought to EDA events.

## Existing Community Organizing

As of April 2026, there are already 10,000+ signatures on an existing community petition opposing the data centre, plus resident-led art protests and zine-making events. This EDA initiative should complement — not duplicate — that existing organizing. Organizers should connect with the existing campaign before launching.

## French Translation

Regina is one of three main urban centres for Saskatchewan's Fransaskois community (~16,000 French-first-language speakers province-wide; Assemblée communautaire fransaskoise secretariat is based in Regina). A French translation of these materials is a recommended future step — particularly the petition, fact sheet, and social copy.
