# Canvasser Guide: Data Centre Petition

**Regina–Wascana NDP | For volunteer use**
*Data and sources reviewed and verified as of April 16, 2026.*

---

## The 30-second opener (door or table)

> "Hi — I'm a volunteer with the Regina–Wascana NDP. We're collecting signatures on a petition about the Bell AI data centre being built just outside Regina. It's a massive facility — 300 megawatts — that could drive up electricity bills for everyone in the province, and it's being built to serve American AI companies. The NDP is calling for a pause on data centre expansion until communities have a real say. Can I tell you more, or would you like to sign?"

If they seem interested, lead with whichever angle connects: energy bills, jobs, governance, or data sovereignty.

---

## What's being built (if they ask)

Bell Canada is building what it calls Canada's largest AI data centre campus — 300 MW on 160 acres in the RM of Sherwood, just south of the city. The announced investment is $1.7 billion. The anchor tenants are CoreWeave and Cerebras — both American AI companies.

Construction is set to start this spring, with operations beginning mid-2027. SaskEnergy is building a new high-pressure natural gas pipeline specifically to feed the facility.

---

## Why it matters locally (use the angle that lands)

**Energy bills:** "In Virginia, where data centres have clustered, electricity bills doubled for regular consumers. Saskatchewan's grid is already strained. A 300 MW facility is a huge new load — and SaskEnergy is building a new gas pipeline just to serve it. Who do you think pays when the grid gets more expensive to run?"

**Jobs:** "Bell says 800 temporary construction jobs and 80 permanent ones. Eighty. For a facility that could reshape the province's energy future. That's not an economic development plan — that's a server farm."

**Governance:** "Four of seven RM of Sherwood council members resigned right after this was announced. We still don't have a full picture of why. When elected representatives walk away, that's a sign the process wasn't working."

**Data sovereignty:** "The anchor tenants are American AI companies — CoreWeave and Cerebras. Under US law — the CLOUD Act — American authorities can compel access to data stored by US companies, even when it's physically in Canada. This isn't theoretical. When the ICC prosecutor issued an arrest warrant for Netanyahu, Trump sanctioned him and Microsoft cut off his email. Microsoft France later admitted under oath to the French Senate that it 'cannot guarantee' it would deny US government requests for data stored in Europe. That's the same legal regime these data centres operate under."

**Climate:** "Saskatchewan's grid runs on roughly 80% fossil fuels — coal and natural gas. A 300 MW data centre on this grid isn't green tech. It's a massive new source of carbon emissions, and it locks us into fossil fuels for decades."

## Who are the tenants?

It's worth knowing who Bell is building this for.

**CoreWeave** started as a cryptocurrency mining company called Atlantic Crypto, founded in 2017 by three hedge fund traders. They pivoted to renting AI server capacity when the money shifted. They went public in early 2025 at a reduced price; the company is deeply unprofitable and carrying roughly $7.5 billion in debt. Major backers include Magnetar Capital — the hedge fund that famously profited from the 2008 financial crisis. About 1,000 employees manage billions in infrastructure. No published policy excluding surveillance or military AI applications from their platform.

**Cerebras** is an AI chip company with active, multi-phase contracts with **DARPA** — the US military's research agency — including building real-time battlefield simulation and electronic warfare systems for the US Navy. The CIA's venture capital arm, **In-Q-Tel**, is an investor. So is **1789 Capital**, the anti-ESG fund co-founded by Rebekah Mercer (major Trump donor) and linked to Peter Thiel — an explicitly "America First" fund. Cerebras's largest customer, accounting for 87% of its revenue in the first half of 2024, was G42, a UAE AI conglomerate flagged by the US Congress for ties to Chinese military and human rights abuses. Cerebras withdrew its IPO in late 2025 amid a federal investigation into that relationship.

These are the companies Saskatchewan is being asked to build public infrastructure for.

## What the NDP is calling for

NDP Leader Avi Lewis has committed to a [**Humans-First AI Policy**](https://lewisisleader.ca/ideas/dignified-work) that includes **pausing the expansion of AI data centres in Canada** — to protect clean water, preserve energy for human health and wellbeing, and give democratic institutions time to catch up to a technology that's been deployed without public debate.

Specific federal levers include:

- Amending the Canadian Environmental Protection Act to regulate data centre impacts
- Withdrawing federal dollars that fund data centre projects
- Levying a tax on data centres
- Or a combination of all of the above

This isn't anti-technology. It's a call for technology that serves people, not the other way around.

## Common objections

**"It's $1.7 billion in investment — that's good for Saskatchewan."**

> "The investment number sounds big, but follow the money. Most of it goes to construction (temporary) and equipment (manufactured elsewhere). Eighty permanent jobs. The energy costs and infrastructure burden stay with us permanently. Investment is only good if the community actually benefits."

**"We need the jobs."**

> "Eighty permanent jobs. Compare that to what the same public investment could create in housing, health care, or renewable energy — sectors that employ people locally, at scale, permanently. This isn't a jobs plan."

**"Data centres are the future — you can't stop progress."**

> "Nobody's saying stop all technology. The NDP supports useful applications of machine learning — things like medical research and solar tracking. What we're questioning is whether a 300 MW facility serving American AI corporations should jump the queue ahead of affordable energy, clean water, and community consent. A pause isn't a ban. It's a chance for our democratic institutions to catch up."

**"Bell says they're using a closed-loop cooling system — no water impact."**

> "That's their claim. But closed-loop systems require significantly more energy to operate than water-cooled ones. On a fossil-fuel grid, that means more emissions. And independent experts have raised questions about what 'closed-loop' actually means at this scale. The point is: we don't have independent assessment yet. That's exactly why there should be a pause."

**"These are big, reputable companies — Bell, CoreWeave, Cerebras."**

> "Bell is the landlord — but the tenants are worth looking at. CoreWeave started as a cryptocurrency mining operation run by hedge fund guys. They're carrying $7.5 billion in debt and went public at a reduced price. Cerebras has active military contracts with DARPA — the US military research agency — building battlefield simulation systems. The CIA's venture fund is an investor. So is an anti-ESG fund linked to Rebekah Mercer and Peter Thiel. These aren't community partners. They're speculative American military-tech firms looking for cheap energy."

**"The provincial government supports it."**

> "The provincial government also plans to run coal plants until 2050 — two decades past the federal phase-out deadline. Provincial support doesn't mean it's in the community's interest. That's what the petition is about: making sure the federal government uses its levers to protect communities, regardless of what the province signs off on."

## The ask

1. **Sign the paper petition** — collect name, address, postal code, email, and phone. All fields help, but don't push if someone hesitates on phone.
2. **Offer to leave a fact sheet** — good for people who want to think about it or share with family.
3. **Note the conversation** — if someone is particularly engaged, enthusiastic, or wants to get involved, flag them for your chapter lead. These are future volunteers.

---

## What to do with signed petitions

- Keep pages flat and dry — don't fold.
- Return to your chapter lead or bring to the next Regina–Wascana NDP event.
- If you collect email addresses, those will be entered into the NDP database by authorized EDA personnel — **do not photograph, screenshot, or store personal data on your personal devices**.

---

## Data custody

The Regina–Wascana NDP electoral district association has custody of all data collected through this petition, governed by the federal NDP Privacy Policy. Personal information is used to contact signatories about NDP activities, shared with riding associations as needed, and never sold. Signatories can opt out at any time by contacting dnc@ndp.ca.

If you have questions about data handling, direct them to your chapter lead.

## Notes

- This is a **party** action — Regina–Wascana NDP. Keep branding at the riding association level.
- The petition asks the **federal government** to act — this is relevant federal jurisdiction (environmental regulation, federal funding, trade and commerce).
- This initiative takes place on **Treaty 4 territory**. Acknowledge this respectfully. Do not speak on behalf of First Nations — if someone raises Indigenous consultation concerns, listen, note them, and share with your chapter lead.
