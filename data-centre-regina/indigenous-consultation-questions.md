# Questions for Local First Nations Contacts

**For the local EDA organizer**
*These are conversation-starters, not a checklist. The goal is to listen and learn, not to extract endorsements.*

---

## Context

The Bell AI data centre campus is being built on 160 acres in the RM of Sherwood — Treaty 4 territory. George Gordon First Nation has signed a procurement and workforce development partnership with Bell, but at least one band member has publicly raised concerns about inadequate prior consultation before that deal was announced.

These questions are for the organizer to bring to local First Nations contacts — elders, council members, community organizers, or anyone the organizer already has a relationship with. The purpose is to understand whether and how First Nations want to engage with this issue, not to assume they should.

---

## Conversation-starters

**On the development itself:**

- Has your Nation or community been consulted about the Bell data centre project? If so, what did that process look like — and did it feel adequate?
- Are there concerns in your community about the data centre's impact on water, land, or energy in the region?
- Were there conditions or commitments made to your Nation as part of the development process? If so, are you comfortable sharing what those are?

**On water and land:**

- Are there water sources, waterways, or lands in the area of the RM of Sherwood development that hold particular significance for your Nation?
- Has anyone in your community raised concerns about the data centre's potential effects on local water quality or supply — even if Bell claims a closed-loop cooling system?
- Are there existing environmental monitoring processes that your Nation leads or participates in that could be relevant here?

**On the partnership with Bell:**

- George Gordon First Nation signed a partnership with Bell focused on procurement and workforce development. Does your Nation or community have a view on that agreement — positive, critical, or otherwise?
- Are there other Nations or communities in the Treaty 4 area that haven't been part of the conversation but should have been?

**On the petition and NDP organizing:**

- Would your Nation or community want to be named or referenced in petition materials about this issue? Or would you prefer not to be associated with party-political materials?
- Is there existing Indigenous-led opposition to or organizing around this data centre that we should know about — and if so, how can we support it rather than duplicate it?
- Is there a better way for the NDP riding association to be a good ally on this issue than a petition? Is there something else you'd want from us?

**On what would actually help:**

- If you could ask the federal government for one thing regarding this data centre, what would it be?
- Is there anything about how settler organizations typically approach these conversations that you'd want us to do differently?

---

## Notes for the organizer

- **Don't bring all of these at once.** Pick the ones that feel right for the relationship and the context. A coffee conversation with two or three questions is more valuable than a formal questionnaire.
- **Listen more than you talk.** If someone wants to tell you their perspective at length, that's the most useful thing that can happen.
- **Don't promise anything.** You're here to learn, not to negotiate. If someone asks what the NDP can do, be honest about what's in your power and what isn't.
- **Report back what you're comfortable sharing.** Some of what you hear may be shared in confidence. Use your judgement about what to pass along and what to hold.
- **If someone says "no thanks"** — to the conversation, to being named, to the petition — respect that fully and don't push.
